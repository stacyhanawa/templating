Good Food

Architecture 100B, Fundamentals of Design

Instructor Roddy Creedon, Spring 2013

An Urban Community Center

Good Food is an urban community center for the education, production, and consumption of sustainable food and agricultural practices. As a mixed-use building, its programs include commissary kitchens, classrooms, and a food hall and research garden.

The site has a heirarchy in orientation, where the dominant grain lies along the main street (Howard), as seen in the site plan below. This spatial organization follows this horizontal grain derived from the site, and drives how both the program and circulation occur inside the building.
